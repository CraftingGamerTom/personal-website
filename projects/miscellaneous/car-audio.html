Car Audio

Overview

I like my music to sound good. I want to be able to feel it and not have a hollow sound to it. Most stock speakers in cars do not have enough feel to them. I decided to change the settings of my speakers to only focus on the high dB rating they are meant for and set up a subwoofer to handle the lower frequencies. I also wanted to replace my stereo (head unit) that had not made it out of the era of cassette tapes.

Solution

I spent some time learning about electical work and speakers and what is required to have a good stereo system. I was shocked at the level of depth that was required to achieve the outcome I wanted. I was not scared away though. I learned a lot and made mistakes a long the way.

I did the entire job myself. Well, honestly, I did have some help installing the fuse near the battery as the fuse I recieved was cheap and difficult to work with. Nevertheless, I wired up the new double DIN head unit (radio) and took apart my car to send wires to the trunk for the subwoofer. The job was suprisingly easy. I even took out the stock flooring in the trunk and built a new, more sturdy flooring of plywood and carpet. I created a hinge system to access my spare tire or car car.

The hardest part, in my opinion, is choosing the hardware. This is where my biggest mistakes took place.

First Subwoofer

My first subwoofer was by far my favorite. Though its life was not very long as I was putting too much power into it and did not have my gain at a healthy level. Amateur mistake. The speaker blew within a year.

Second Subwoofer

The second subwoofer I have barely lasted over a year as well. This time this seemed to be a manufacturer issue. Not long after my warrenty expired the main spring in the hardware that allowed the speaker to actually make any meaningful sound failed. If I were to press on the speaker it would work, that would not be ideal though. The work to replace the spring was not worth it and it is likely the speaker would not function properly after. I had to buy a new one.

Third Subwoofer

My latest speaker is doing a fantastic job so far. They do not last forever but it has been over 2 years. I'll keep you updated.
