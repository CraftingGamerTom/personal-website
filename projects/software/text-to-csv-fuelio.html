Text To CSV (Fuelio)

The Problem

I had gathered data over a few years every time I accompanied my car to a gas station. I collected the mileage since last fill up, money spent, purchased gallon quantity, and the date. I wanted to back up, track, and analyze the data over time.

After dabbling with the idea of writing an app to handle this task, simply to practice problem solving, I checked the internet for something that already existed.

I found Fuelio, and application that synced with my google drive and had more analytical feedback than I had thought of ever needing. Though now I can't believe I lived without it! I needed to convert my manually recorded data into data that Fuelio could understand.

Solution

I ran my data through a parser program I had written. Though it did require me to fix a few formatting issues in the hand typed data set, it was mostly smooth. The program took the data and created a comma seperated value list (csv) that I was then able to build a data set in the csv file Fuelio maintains data through. It was as simple as replacing the data file on my phone with the one I created. I, of course, set up Google Drive to maintain a copy of the data and automatically sync with the phone. I was able to simply load the data from Google drive. I did not need to manually change any files on my phone.

It was a quick and easy task that continues to pay off. I am able to determine fuel consumption, I have a history of gas prices from experience, and much more. No, fuelio is not paying me anything, I have enjoyed the ease of use their app has.
